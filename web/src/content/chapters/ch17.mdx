There is no golem to forge from a formula here. Chapter 17 has no models,
because its subject is the one thing no model contains: judgment. A golem
does exactly what its structure tells it to, with no sense of whether the
question was worth asking or the answer worth trusting. That sense stays with
you, and this chapter is where the course hands it back.

## The horoscope problem

A horoscope is unfalsifiable by design — vague enough to feel true whatever
happens. A surprising amount of statistical practice works the same way. A
model that can absorb any result explains nothing; a workflow with enough
researcher degrees of freedom will find a significant effect in noise; a
default prior smuggled in without thought is a wish dressed as knowledge. The
antidote is everything you built: predict before you reveal, simulate before
you fit, draw the causal graph, and check the model against data it has never
seen.

The calibration number is not a grade. It is a mirror: it shows where your
sense of uncertainty tracked the truth and where it ran too narrow, which is
the most common way to be wrong. A well-calibrated forecaster who says "70%"
is right about seventy percent of the time — no more, and no less. That habit,
more than any single model, is what the course was training.

## What the golems cannot do

Each golem in your bestiary has a horizon set by its structure, not its
sample size. The Gaussian golem predicts height from weight and can never tell
you whether feeding a child changes its adult height — that is a causal
question its structure does not encode. The compass ranks models for
prediction and is silent on cause. The multilevel golems pool across clusters
and assume the clusters are exchangeable, which is sometimes exactly wrong.
Knowing which golem to distrust, and when to reach for another, is the wisdom
the workshop cannot forge for you. It is the part you supply.

## Forge it

The three bench drills close the loop. Read your own calibration and diagnose
its worst chapter; name a golem's structural limit and the golem you would
need instead; and find one place in your own work where a default still stands
in for a scientific model you have not written down. Finish them and the
Workshop Master Seal is stamped — not on a creature, but on you.
